GigJam is an app created by Microsoft that allows users to securely share work with people in their network. GigJam removes boundaries between apps, businesses and devices. With GigJam you can summon, divvy up and share what you want, for only as long as you need.

As a member of the design team, I engaged in internal design and subsequent redesign of the UX flow and architecture of the GigJam app, with several of my concepts being adopted by the UX team.

I designed the onboarding for Microsoft's GigJam app. My designs were included in the January 2017 public release of the app.

I worked with the PM team to come up with a set of features we wanted to highlight in the onboarding and then I created the graphics to visually represent these concepts. I have been interested in product onboarding for a while and I tried to incorporate best practices into this design.

Initial designs included an interactive experience, so the user was gradually introduced to the concepts of the app while they were actually using it. Through a back and forth reiteration process with the PMs, developers, and myself, the onboarding was pared down to simple steps that were easy to follow. The visuals I designed aimed to ease users in as seamlessly as possible and clearly explain the functionality and features of the app.

The finished product allows the users to swipe through a set of screens that are visually appealing and get the message across quickly, so nobody gets bogged down before entering the app.
